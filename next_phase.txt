# What Pilots Actually Like

After reviewing pilot forums, app reviews, airline discussions, and aviation weather research, several themes appear repeatedly.

## 1. Pilots Love Visual Weather

Windy became popular because weather became intuitive.

Instead of:

* METAR
* TAF
* SIGMET
* AIRMET
* Winds Aloft

Pilots can instantly see:

* wind flow
* cloud layers
* precipitation
* pressure systems

Users consistently praise apps that simplify complex weather information visually. ([MWM][1])

### Why this matters

Pilots don't want weather data.

They want answers:

* Can I go?
* Where is turbulence?
* Which altitude is best?
* What route is safest?

---

## 2. Pilots Hate Information Overload

One recurring complaint:

> Weather apps keep adding features but become cluttered.

Users complain when apps become bloated, harder to navigate, or require too many taps. ([App Store][2])

### Opportunity

Build:

**One screen = mission answer**

Example:

Current Route:
LFST → EGLL

System instantly shows:

* fuel penalty: +312 kg
* turbulence: moderate
* icing risk: none
* recommended altitude: FL360
* ETA gain: +7 min

No hunting through 15 overlays.

# What Pilots Hate Most

## 1. "I Have To Interpret Everything Myself"

Current workflow:

Pilot sees:

* winds
* SIGMETs
* turbulence layers
* cloud forecasts
* NOTAMs

Then must mentally combine everything.

This creates workload.

Research and operational discussions show that weather apps provide data but often stop short of actionable decision support. ([Aviation International News][3])

### Massive Gap

Your system could answer:

> "What is the operational impact?"

instead of

> "Here are 20 weather layers."

---

## 2. Fuel Predictions Lack Transparency

Pilots often question why route planners recommend certain fuel burns or altitudes. There are frequent discussions around unexpected fuel calculations and unclear assumptions. ([Reddit][4])

### Huge Opportunity

Show:

"Why"

instead of just:

"Result"

Example:

Recommended FL390

Reason:

* Tailwind +42 knots
* Lower turbulence index
* Fuel saving 184 kg
* Time saving 5 minutes

This creates trust.

---

## 3. Poor Offline Capability

Many pilots operate where connectivity is unreliable.

A common concern is whether an app remains useful when network coverage drops. ([pilotgpt.com][5])

### Opportunity

Your app could cache:

* route weather
* wind grids
* fuel calculations
* alternate airports

for the entire flight.
